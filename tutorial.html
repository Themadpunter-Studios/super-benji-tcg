Learn Fast

How To Play In 12 Slides

This short walkthrough teaches the game step by step. We use real cards so you can see what each rule looks like during play.

Interactive Tutorial

1. Your Goal

Your goal is simple: lower your opponent's health to 0.

  • You and your opponent both start with 20 health.
  • Your cards let you attack, block, and use effects.
  • When a player's health becomes 0, the game ends right away.

2. Know Card Roles

Each card type has a different job.

  • Characters can attack and block.
  • Actions happen once, then usually go to discard.
  • Locations and Artifacts stay in play and keep helping you.

3. Start Of Game Setup

Get the game ready before the first turn.

  • Shuffle your deck and draw your opening hand of 3 cards.
  • Set both players to 20 health.
  • Pick who goes first, then start with an empty board.

4. Building Your Deck

A deck has 27 cards.

  • You can use up to 3 copies of the same card in your deck.
  • If a card's type line includes Unique, you can only use 1 copy of that card.
  • Deck building is about choosing your 27 cards before the game starts.

5. On Your Turn

On your turn, you can play as many cards as you want from your hand.

  • All your cards heal to their maximum health.
  • All cards you own give you actions to use.
  • If a card is in your hand, you can play it - no actions needed.
  • You can play any card type this way: Character, Action, Location, or Artifact.
  • You can play as many cards as you want during your turn.
  • Cards give you actions to use immediately, and then again at the start of every turn.

6. Use Actions For Abilities

You spend actions when using abilities that show action symbols.

  • Look at the symbols shown in an ability's text.
  • Spend matching symbols from your action pool to activate that ability.
  • Wild action symbols can pay for any one required symbol.
  • If an ability doesn't have any action symbols, you don't need to spend any actions to use it.

7. Parts Of A Card

Each card has parts that tell you what it is and what it does.

  • Name: the card title at the top.
  • Type line: says Character, Action, Location, or Artifact, as well as more information about the card.
  • Actions: Shows how many actions the card gives you when it is played, and at the start of your turn.
  • Rules text: explains effects, abilities, and costs.
  • Keywords: short rule words like Speed, Flying, and Reach.
  • Stats: Character cards show attack and health numbers at the bottom of the card text in the format Attack/Health.

8. Attack Phase: Declaring Attacks

Pick which characters will attack this turn.

  • When a character attacks, it becomes exhausted. Characters can't attack or block if they are exhausted.
  • Attacking cards will deal damage to your opponent's health unless blocked.
  • Characters cannot attack on the turn they are played unless they have Speed.
  • After attacks are declared, the defending player chooses blockers.

9. Attack Phase: Defending And Blocking

The defending player chooses blockers.

  • Choose characters to block attackers and stop damage to yourself.
  • The attacker and blocker deal damage to each other. If any character has 0 or less health, it is destroyed.
  • If an attacker is not blocked, it deals damage to your opponent.
  • Characters heal at the start of your turn.

10. Keywords And What They Mean

Keywords are short rule words. They exist to keep card text shorter.

  • Speed: this character can attack on the turn it is played.
  • Flying: only characters with Flying or Reach can block it.
  • Reach: this character can block Flying attackers.
  • Vigilance: this character does not exhaust when it attacks.
  • Lethal: any damage this character deals to a character destroys it.
  • Unique: only one copy of this card can be in your deck.

11. Turn Wrap-Up

Finish the turn, then pass to the other player.

  • Resolve all "end of turn" effects.
  • Do cleanup steps required by your rules.
  • Then the next player's turn begins.

12. Closing The Match

The game ends when a win condition is met.

  • Main win condition: reduce your opponent's health to 0.
  • If a card gives another win condition, follow that card text.
  • As soon as a win condition is met, the game ends.
